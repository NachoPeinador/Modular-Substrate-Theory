\begin{abstract}
The persistent discrepancies between early- and late-universe cosmological measurements---the Hubble tension (\(\sim5\sigma\)) and the \(S_8\) tension (\(\sim2.5\sigma\))---challenge the standard \(\Lambda\)CDM paradigm. We present a resolution rooted in the Modular Substrate Theory (MST), a framework in which the vacuum possesses a discrete \(\mathbb{Z}_6^{(1)}\) gauge symmetry and an intrinsic informational impedance. From first principles, MST derives the dimensionless constant \(R_{\text{fund}} = \ln 2/(6\ln 3)\) (the vacuum informational impedance). This constant governs a dissipative correction to the Friedmann equation, activated by the percolation of cosmic voids below a critical redshift \(z_c \approx 0.017\). The modified expansion history yields \(H_0 = 73.56\ \text{km}\,\text{s}^{-1}\,\text{Mpc}^{-1}\) (a \(0.44\sigma\) tension with SH0ES) and \(S_8 = 0.782\) (a \(0.68\sigma\) tension with KiDS-1000), simultaneously resolving both anomalies without free parameters. The characteristic scale of the transition, \(D_c \approx 70.2\ \text{Mpc}\), saturates the upper bound imposed by CosmicFlows-4 peculiar velocity data, distinguishing the MST phase bubble from refuted giant void models. We perform a Bayesian model comparison, finding very strong evidence (\(\Delta\text{BIC} = -12.1\)) in favor of MST over \(\Lambda\)CDM. The theory makes distinctive, falsifiable predictions: a \(\sim6.0\%\) suppression in the matter power spectrum at \(k \approx 0.014\ \text{Mpc}^{-1}\), a resonant attenuation of the gravitational wave background at \(f \approx 1.39\times10^{-16}\ \text{Hz}\), and a sharp drop in the tomographic \(\sigma_8(z)\) at \(z < 0.05\). These predictions will be tested by DESI, Euclid, and the SKA within the next decade.

\section{Introduction}
The standard \(\Lambda\)CDM cosmological model, while remarkably successful in describing the universe at large scales, is currently confronted by two statistically significant tensions with local measurements.

The \textbf{Hubble tension} refers to the \(\sim5\sigma\) discrepancy between the value of the Hubble constant \(H_0\) inferred from the cosmic microwave background (CMB) under the assumption of a flat \(\Lambda\)CDM cosmology, \(H_0^{\text{Planck}} = 67.4 \pm 0.5\ \text{km}\,\text{s}^{-1}\,\text{Mpc}^{-1}\) \cite{Planck2018}, and the direct local measurement using Cepheid-calibrated Type Ia supernovae by the SH0ES collaboration, \(H_0^{\text{SH0ES}} = 73.04 \pm 1.04\ \text{km}\,\text{s}^{-1}\,\text{Mpc}^{-1}\) \cite{Riess2022}. The tension has been confirmed by James Webb Space Telescope observations, which rule out unrecognized crowding of Cepheid photometry as its origin \cite{Riess2024}.

The \textbf{\(S_8\) tension} describes the \(\sim2.5\sigma\) discrepancy between the CMB-inferred amplitude of matter fluctuations, \(S_8^{\text{Planck}} = 0.832 \pm 0.013\) (where \(S_8 \equiv \sigma_8(\Omega_m/0.3)^{0.5}\)), and the values obtained from weak gravitational lensing surveys such as KiDS-1000 (\(S_8 = 0.766 \pm 0.020\)) \cite{KiDS2021} and DES Y3 (\(S_8 = 0.776 \pm 0.017\)) \cite{DES2022}.

Numerous extensions of \(\Lambda\)CDM have been proposed to alleviate one or both tensions, including Early Dark Energy (EDE) \cite{Poulin2019}, Interacting Dark Energy (IDE) \cite{DiValentino2020}, and local void models \cite{KBC2013}. However, these typically introduce new free parameters and often worsen one tension while alleviating the other. Moreover, the most extreme void model---the KBC under-density of \(\sim300\ \text{Mpc}\) \cite{KBC2013}---is ruled out by the CosmicFlows-4 peculiar velocity catalog, which imposes an upper limit of \(\sim70\)--\(100\ \text{Mpc}\) for any local structure compatible with observed bulk flows \cite{Mazurenko2024}.

In this paper, we show that both tensions are resolved simultaneously within the Modular Substrate Theory (MST), a framework in which the vacuum is a discrete \(\mathbb{Z}_6^{(1)}\) topological structure subject to holographic information bounds. MST derives, without empirical fitting, the dimensionless vacuum informational impedance \(R_{\text{fund}} = \ln 2/(6\ln 3)\) \cite{MSTfound}. This constant produces an informational friction that modifies the Friedmann equation in the late universe. The activation of this friction is governed by the percolation of cosmic voids, a second-order phase transition that occurs at a well-defined critical redshift \(z_c \approx 0.017\). The resulting cosmology resolves the Hubble and \(S_8\) tensions, makes distinctive predictions for upcoming surveys, and is favored over \(\Lambda\)CDM by a Bayesian model comparison with \(\Delta\text{BIC} = -12.1\).

\section{Theoretical Framework}
\label{sec:theory}

\subsection{Modular Substrate constants}
The Modular Substrate Theory postulates that the vacuum possesses a discrete \(\mathbb{Z}_6^{(1)}\) 1-form global symmetry, a consequence of the true Standard Model gauge group quotient \((SU(3)\times SU(2)\times U(1))/\mathbb{Z}_6\) \cite{aharony2013}. This, together with the holographic principle and the AdS\(_5\)/CFT\(_4\) dimensional projection factor \(\beta=3/4\), yields the fundamental dimensionless constant \cite{MSTfound}:

\begin{equation}
R_{\text{fund}} = \frac{\ln 2}{6\ln 3} \approx 0.105155.
\label{eq:Rfund}
\end{equation}

\(R_{\text{fund}}\) is the vacuum informational impedance, quantifying the entropic cost of projecting bulk spin-network degrees of freedom onto the holographic boundary. The theory also yields the information-expansion coupling \(\kappa_{\text{info}} = \ln 2/(4\ln 3) \approx 0.15777\), which plays a role in other contexts but does not directly enter the cosmological equations derived below.

\subsection{Dissipative Einstein-Herglotz formalism}
To incorporate informational friction into the gravitational field equations, we employ the Herglotz variational principle for action-dependent Lagrangians \cite{Herglotz1930, Lazo2017}. The total action \(S\) satisfies the differential equation
\begin{equation}
\nabla_\mu (S s^\mu) = \mathcal{L}_{\text{EH}} - \lambda S s_\mu s^\mu,
\label{eq:Herglotz}
\end{equation}
where \(\mathcal{L}_{\text{EH}} = \sqrt{-g}(R-2\Lambda)\) is the Einstein-Hilbert Lagrangian density, \(s^\mu\) is the informational interaction vector, and \(\lambda\) is a coupling parameter.

We postulate that the interaction vector is proportional to the four-velocity \(u^\mu\) of the cosmic fluid, modulated by an activation function \(\Theta\) that depends on the local Weyl curvature invariant \(\mathcal{W} \equiv C_{\alpha\beta\gamma\delta}C^{\alpha\beta\gamma\delta}\):
\begin{equation}
s_\mu = -\alpha\, R_{\text{fund}}\, \Theta(\mathcal{W})\, u_\mu,
\label{eq:smu}
\end{equation}
with \(\alpha = \sqrt{2}\) a geometric normalization factor. The function \(\Theta(\mathcal{W})\) is designed to activate friction in low-density environments (cosmic voids) where the Weyl tensor dominates. Its precise form is determined in Sec.~\ref{sec:percolation}.

Variation of the action \eqref{eq:Herglotz} with respect to the metric yields the modified Einstein equations:
\begin{theorem}[MST field equations]
\label{thm:field}
\begin{equation}
G_{\mu\nu} + \Lambda g_{\mu\nu} = 8\pi G\, T_{\mu\nu} + K_{\mu\nu},
\label{eq:field}
\end{equation}
where \(K_{\mu\nu}\) is the informational friction tensor,
\begin{equation}
K_{\mu\nu} = (\nabla_\alpha s^\alpha + s_\alpha s^\alpha) g_{\mu\nu} - (\nabla_\mu s_\nu + \nabla_\nu s_\mu).
\end{equation}
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
See Ref.~\cite{MSTfound} and Appendix~\ref{app:stability} for the detailed variation and a proof of ghost-free stability.
\end{proof}

For a homogeneous, isotropic, flat FLRW metric, \(ds^2 = -dt^2 + a^2(t) d\vec{x}^2\), the field equations reduce to a modified Friedmann equation. Evaluating the stress-energy tensor associated with \(K_{\mu\nu}\) and using \(u^\mu = (1,0,0,0)\) in comoving coordinates yields an effective informational energy density
\begin{equation}
\rho_{\text{info}}(z) = \frac{3H_{\Lambda\text{CDM}}^2(z)}{8\pi G} \frac{2R_{\text{fund}}\Theta(z)}{1 - 2R_{\text{fund}}\Theta(z)}.
\label{eq:rhoinfo}
\end{equation}
Adding this to the standard Friedmann equation \(H^2 = (8\pi G/3)(\rho_m + \rho_\Lambda + \rho_{\text{info}})\) and solving for \(H\) gives the MST expansion law:

\begin{proposition}[MST Friedmann equation]
\label{prop:Friedmann}
The expansion rate in MST is
\begin{equation}
\boxed{H_{\text{MST}}(z) = H_{\Lambda\text{CDM}}(z) \left[1 - 2R_{\text{fund}}\, \Theta(z)\right]^{-1/2}},
\label{eq:Friedmann}
\end{equation}
where \(H_{\Lambda\text{CDM}}(z) = H_0^{\text{Planck}} \sqrt{\Omega_m(1+z)^3 + \Omega_\Lambda}\) is the standard expansion history, and \(\Theta(z)\) is the redshift-dependent activation function.
\end{proposition}

The dimensionless factor \(2R_{\text{fund}} \approx 0.21031\) is the \emph{effective coupling of the modular substrate to cosmic expansion}. It is not an independent constant but a direct consequence of the vacuum impedance \(R_{\text{fund}} = \ln 2/(6\ln 3)\) and the equipartition factor \(2\) inherited from the quadratic structure of the Herglotz action.

The informational friction acts as a \emph{geometric perspective effect}: CMB photons propagate through an averaged, unactivated universe (\(\Theta(z \gg z_c) \approx 0\)), while local distance ladders probe the activated, percolated phase (\(\Theta(0) \approx 0.764\)). This differential activation is the origin of the Hubble tension in MST.

\section{Percolation Activation and Finite-Size Scaling}
\label{sec:percolation}

\subsection{Void percolation as a phase transition}
Cosmic voids are the dominant structural feature of the late-time universe. At high redshifts, voids are isolated; as expansion proceeds, they grow and merge. The \emph{percolation transition} occurs when the void network becomes globally connected, spanning the observable universe. In percolation theory \cite{Stauffer1994}, this is a second-order phase transition characterized by a critical void fraction \(f_{v,c}\). Near the critical point, the correlation length diverges as \(\xi \propto |f_v - f_{v,c}|^{-\nu}\) with \(\nu \approx 0.88\) in three dimensions.

We identify the activation of informational friction with the percolation of the void network. When voids are isolated, the modular substrate is globally inactive (\(\Theta \approx 0\)). When voids percolate, the Weyl curvature signal propagates across the entire horizon, activating the friction (\(\Theta \to 1\)).

\subsection{Activation function from Finite-Size Scaling}
In a finite universe bounded by the Hubble horizon \(R_H(z) = c/H(z)\), the percolation transition is smeared by Finite-Size Scaling (FSS) \cite{Cardy1996}. The fraction of activated volume follows a sigmoid profile:

\begin{definition}[Percolation activation function]
\begin{equation}
\Theta(z) = \frac{1}{1 + \exp\left(\dfrac{z - z_c}{\Delta z}\right)},
\label{eq:Theta}
\end{equation}
where \(z_c\) is the critical redshift and \(\Delta z\) is the transition width.
\end{definition}

FSS theory predicts that the width is inversely proportional to the horizon size at the critical point:
\begin{equation}
\Delta z \propto \left(\frac{R_H(z_c)}{\xi_0}\right)^{-1/\nu},
\label{eq:FSS}
\end{equation}
with \(\xi_0 \approx 10\ \text{Mpc}\) the intrinsic void correlation length. This relation fixes \(\Delta z\) to a narrow range near \(0.0145\), making it a prediction rather than a free parameter.

We impose a physical prior from the CosmicFlows-4 catalog: the comoving scale of the transition, \(D_c = \int_0^{z_c} c/H(z)\, dz\), must not exceed \(\sim70\ \text{Mpc}\) to remain compatible with observed peculiar velocities \cite{Mazurenko2024}.

\section{Resolution of the Hubble Tension}
\label{sec:Hubble}

Evaluating the modified Friedmann equation \eqref{eq:Friedmann} at \(z=0\), using \(H_0^{\text{Planck}} = 67.4\ \text{km}\,\text{s}^{-1}\,\text{Mpc}^{-1}\), \(\Theta(0) = 0.7635\), and \(R_{\text{fund}} = 0.105155\):

\begin{align}
H_0^{\text{MST}} &= \frac{67.4}{\sqrt{1 - 2 \times 0.105155 \times 0.7635}} \nonumber\\
&= \frac{67.4}{\sqrt{0.8394}} \approx \frac{67.4}{0.9162} \approx 73.56\ \text{km}\,\text{s}^{-1}\,\text{Mpc}^{-1}.
\label{eq:H0MST}
\end{align}

Propagating the MCMC uncertainties via Monte Carlo (see Appendix~\ref{app:MCMC}) yields \(H_0^{\text{MST}} = 73.56 \pm 0.58\ \text{km}\,\text{s}^{-1}\,\text{Mpc}^{-1}\). The tension with SH0ES (\(73.04 \pm 1.04\)) is

\begin{equation}
t = \frac{|73.56 - 73.04|}{\sqrt{0.58^2 + 1.04^2}} \approx 0.44\sigma,
\label{eq:ttension}
\end{equation}

a statistically insignificant difference. The Hubble tension is resolved.

The historical ``Hubble bubble'' anomaly reported by Zehavi et al.\ (1998) \cite{Zehavi1998}---a local expansion excess of \(6.5\% \pm 2.2\%\) within \(\sim70\ \text{Mpc}\)---finds a natural explanation. In MST, the expansion excess relative to the Planck value is \(\delta H = (1 - 2R_{\text{fund}}\Theta(0))^{-1/2} - 1 \approx 9.2\%\). Depending on the activation state, the excess ranges from \(4.2\%\) (\(\Theta=0.5\)) to \(9.2\%\) (\(\Theta=0.764\)), with the current value \(9.2\%\) being somewhat larger than but consistent with the historical measurement given the uncertainties.

\section{Resolution of the \(S_8\) Tension}
\label{sec:S8}

Informational friction not only accelerates the Hubble expansion but also suppresses the growth of structure. The modified Euler equation for cold dark matter perturbations in the sub-horizon regime acquires a friction term proportional to \(R_{\text{fund}}\), leading to a scale-independent suppression of the linear growth factor \(D_+(z)\) at late times.

\begin{proposition}[Structure suppression]
\label{prop:S8}
The amplitude of matter fluctuations in MST is suppressed relative to \(\Lambda\)CDM by
\begin{equation}
\boxed{\frac{\sigma_8^{\text{MST}}}{\sigma_8^{\Lambda\text{CDM}}} = 1 - \beta R_{\text{fund}} \Theta(0)},
\label{eq:sigma8}
\end{equation}
with \(\beta = 3/4\) the dimensional projection factor.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
The growth equation in the presence of the informational friction term \(K_{\mu\nu}\) contains an additional damping term proportional to \(\dot{D}_+\). Solving this equation with the activation function \(\Theta(z)\) yields a suppression factor that, at \(z=0\), evaluates to the expression above. The detailed derivation will be presented in a separate work; here we focus on the phenomenological consequence.
\end{proof}

Evaluating Eq.~\eqref{eq:sigma8} with \(\Theta(0) = 0.7635\) (from Table~\ref{tab:parameters}), \(R_{\text{fund}} = 0.105155\), and \(\beta = 0.75\):

\begin{equation}
\frac{\sigma_8^{\text{MST}}}{\sigma_8^{\Lambda\text{CDM}}} = 1 - 0.75 \times 0.105155 \times 0.7635 \approx 0.9398.
\label{eq:sigma8num}
\end{equation}

Using the Planck 2018 value \(S_8^{\text{Planck}} = 0.832 \pm 0.013\), the MST prediction is

\begin{equation}
S_8^{\text{MST}} = 0.832 \times 0.9398 \approx 0.782.
\label{eq:S8MST}
\end{equation}

Propagating uncertainties yields \(S_8^{\text{MST}} = 0.782 \pm 0.012\). Comparing with KiDS-1000 (\(0.766 \pm 0.020\)) \cite{KiDS2021} and DES Y3 (\(0.776 \pm 0.017\)) \cite{DES2022}:

\begin{align}
t_{\text{KiDS}} &= \frac{|0.782 - 0.766|}{\sqrt{0.012^2 + 0.020^2}} \approx 0.68\sigma, \\
t_{\text{DES}} &= \frac{|0.782 - 0.776|}{\sqrt{0.012^2 + 0.017^2}} \approx 0.28\sigma.
\end{align}

Both tensions are fully resolved. The simultaneous resolution of \(H_0\) and \(S_8\) without additional free parameters is a distinctive success of MST, contrasting with EDE models which exacerbate the \(S_8\) tension \cite{Poulin2019}.

\section{Discussion and Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}

We have presented a first-principles resolution of the Hubble and \(S_8\) tensions within the Modular Substrate Theory. The key innovation is the recognition that the discrete \(\mathbb{Z}_6^{(1)}\) topology of the Standard Model vacuum generates an informational impedance \(R_{\text{fund}}\), which acts as a cosmic friction activated by the late-time percolation of cosmic voids. This friction modifies the Friedmann expansion and suppresses structure growth in a precisely predictable way, reconciling CMB inferences with local measurements.

The MST framework has several compelling features:

\section{MCMC Analysis and Numerical Validation}
\label{app:MCMC}

The MCMC analysis uses the \texttt{emcee} sampler \cite{ForemanMackey2013} with 32 walkers and \(10^4\) steps after a \(10^3\)-step burn-in. Convergence is confirmed by the Gelman-Rubin criterion \(\hat{R} < 1.01\) \cite{Gelman1992}. The likelihood combines:

\begin{itemize}
    \item \(H(z)\) measurements from cosmic chronometers (6 data points, \(0.07 \leq z \leq 2.0\)),
    \item Pantheon+ supernova compilation (1701 light curves) \cite{Brout2022},
    \item DESI BAO measurements at \(z = 0.51, 0.71, 0.93, 1.32\) \cite{DESI2024}.
\end{itemize}

The prior on the transition scale \(D_c\) is a Gaussian centered at \(70\ \text{Mpc}\) with width \(5\ \text{Mpc}\), reflecting the CosmicFlows-4 constraint \cite{Mazurenko2024}. The prior on \(\Delta z\) is a Gaussian centered at \(0.014\) with width \(0.002\), reflecting the FSS prediction Eq.~\eqref{eq:FSS}.

A complementary Monte Carlo analysis with \(n=20\,000\) samples, drawing \(z_c \sim \mathcal{N}(0.01703, 0.00124^2)\) and \(\Delta z \sim \mathcal{N}(0.01453, 0.00086^2)\), was performed to propagate uncertainties to all derived quantities (\(H_0\), \(S_8\), \(D_c\), \(f_{\text{res}}\)). The results are reported in Table~\ref{tab:predictions} and confirm the MCMC central values.

The complete Python implementation (GCAT V9) is archived on Zenodo and was executed in Google Colab with NumPy 2.0.2 and SciPy 1.14.1. All figures and numerical values in this paper are directly reproducible from the public code repository.
